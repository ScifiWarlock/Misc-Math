\section{Participating Parties}
This contract is written by \textbf{Omkar Tasgaonkar} on the \textbf{8th of July, 2026} for the signatory \textbf{Vaishal Karpuram} regarding \textbf{caffeine use in college}. This contract is binding and expires at the exact date and time specified below, after which the signatory and the drafter will make the promised financial exchange(s).

\medskip
\section{Payment}
The sum of \textbf{\$100} is to be the value of payment. If one party is not able to procure the sum, then it is up to the discretion of the other party on what to do; however, interest may NOT be charged on the payment amount.

\smallskip
\subsubsection{Loss Clause}
The sum must be paid in either a cash amount or online bank-transfer (Zelle or Apple pay) in a timely manner on \textbf{Saturday, June 16, 2029}, unless the contract is broken before the specified date under the conditions stated below. In this case, the signatory must offer payment to the drafter any time before the date of \textbf{June 16, 2029} with no method of reversal.

\medskip
\section{Definitions}
\subsubsection{Caffeine}
Caffeine is defined as the chemical as per IUPAC standards: \textbf{1,3,7-trimethylpurine-2,6-dione} possessing the chemical formula	
$C_8H_{10}N_4O_2.$

\smallskip
\subsubsection{Consumption}
Caffeine consumption is defined as the purposeful consumption of this chemical through either liquid or solid mediums, and will result in the activation of the loss clause; for example:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Black/Unsweetened Coffee
\item Any Variety of Latte
\item Cappucino/Frappucino/Americano/Espresso
\item Any Variety of Energy Drinks
\item Any Pills containing Caffeine not taken for medical purposes
\end{enumerate}

\smallskip
Specific items will not result in the activation of the loss clause such as any variety of tea and any variety of SPECIFICALLY desserts that contain caffeine (for example but not limited to tiramisu cake). 

\subsubsection{Arbitration Clause}
Note that this is not an exhaustive list. If the drafter claims of caffeine use, then it is up to their discretion to call upon the terms of this contract. In the case that the signatory does not except, both sides must procure two people each for temporary consultation roles to determine whether the consumption of caffeine occured. If a majority agrees upon this (3/4 people) then the terms of the contract apply as stated in \textbf{Section 2 Loss Clause}.

\subsubsection{Recreational Purposes/Activities}
Recreational activities are defined as any activities engaged for non-academic purposes. To further clarify, this means any activities not involving any:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Class Assignments: Items with a set deadline, worth a grade for a class in a quarter, assigned by any faculty member (including TAs)
\item Study Sessions: Sessions related to an academic class taken in a quarter under supervision of a faculty member or independently undertaken alone or along with other students
\begin{enumerate}
\item Students need not be from UCLA. Students are defined as any person engaged in any academic or extracurricular pursuit.
\end{enumerate}
\item Extracurricular Sessions: Sessions related to an organization outside of academic classes
\begin{enumerate}
\item The organization may be academic in nature
\item The organization may be non-academic (which includes cultural clubs, musical clubs, service clubs, etc.)
\item The organization may be project-based in nature (eg Engineering Clubs)
\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Caffeinated Foods}
The agreement shall treat solid foods (those typically eaten by utensils or hands) differently than liquid items (which are items that do not require utensils or hands for consumptions). Restrictions on liquid items shall be greater than solid foods. Solid foods containing caffeine consumed for recreational purposes (as defined in the previous subsection) shall not activate the loss clause. Liquid items however will result in activation even if their consumption was for recreational purposes.

\subsubsection{Arbitration}
If the drafter and signatory do not agree upon a potential violation and activation of the loss clause, then they must utilize the \textbf{Arbitration Clause} to come to a conclusion.

\section{Agreement}
Signatures below remain binding till payment is succesfully completed. Both participating parties agree that they will honor this contract once signed. It is up to the discretion of the drafter and signatory whether to change terms in this contract after signing, but such an action requires the concensus of both parties. If both parties agree to end the contract prior to the execution of payment, then the contract is null and void.
